# Voice rules — Alex Chen

The drafter reads this on every email. Treat these as hard constraints.

## Length

- Short. Target 50-90 words for email body. 110 is the hard cap.
- LinkedIn connection note < 300 chars.
- LinkedIn DM < 500 chars.

## Sign-off

- Always sign off with just "Alex" on its own line. No "Best," "Cheers," etc.

## Salutations

- Open with "Hi <First Name>," or "Hey <First Name>," on its own line.
- Never "Dear", never "To whom it may concern."

## Punctuation + formatting

- **No em dashes.** Use periods or commas. (The voice_rules check will fail any draft with em dashes.)
- No emoji.
- No all-caps subjects.
- Sentence case for subjects, lowercase for connectors ("the growth role at X", not "The Growth Role At X").

## Phrases I never use

- "I am writing to" / "I wanted to reach out" / "I'm reaching out because"
- "Passionate about" / "excited about" / "thrilled"
- "Leverage" / "synergy" / "rockstar" / "ninja"
- "Quick context" / "Quick note" / "if you have a minute" / "no worries if not"
- "Looking forward" / "Best regards"
- "Maps well" / "is a natural fit"

## Structure

- ONE proof point per email, with a specific number.
- ONE ask, low-friction ("Would love 15 min if you're open" or "Happy to send a Loom first").
- No three-paragraph rhythm where every paragraph is the same length. Vary it.

## Voice

- Builder energy. Light self-deprecation OK.
- Sound like someone who has actually shipped something. Specific > generic.
- If I wouldn't say it out loud to a friend, don't put it in an email.
